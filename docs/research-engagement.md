# Engagement & Retention Research

## Key Stats
- Mental health apps: **3.9% retention at 30 days**
- Proactive outreach = **2.4x better retention** vs passive-only
- Onboarding drop-off spikes above 4 questions
- Daylio's 2-second check-in has among the highest retention in category

## Top 3 Abandonment Reasons
1. "I feel better, don't need this" — no value proposition for good days
2. Content exhaustion / repetitiveness — feels like a textbook after 2 weeks
3. No felt relationship — each session feels like starting over

## What To Build (priority order)

### 1. Daylio-style 2-Second Mood Check-in [VERY HIGH impact]
- One tap for mood (5 emoji faces)
- Optional activity tags (preset icons)
- Optional note
- Visible accumulation (mood calendar)
- Drives daily habit with near-zero friction

### 2. Onboarding Flow [VERY HIGH impact]
4 phases, 3-5 minutes total:
1. Warm welcome — set expectations ("I'm not a therapist but...")
2. Low-stakes interaction — "One word for how you feel right now?"
3. Light personalization — 2-3 questions max (what brought you, when free)
4. First micro-win — deliver ONE useful technique immediately (breathing exercise)
End with: "I'll check in tomorrow at [time]. Sound good?"

### 3. Re-engagement Emails [HIGH impact, LOW effort]
- Day 3: Warm check-in, no guilt
- Day 7: Reference something from their last conversation
- Day 14: "We're still here" + micro-action offer
- Never: "You haven't logged in!" or streak-breaking language

### 4. Good Day Experience [MEDIUM impact]
- Savoring prompts: "What went well today and WHY?"
- Micro-wins tracking: "I did a thing" tap
- Strength spotting: "What personal strength did you use today?"
- NOT: toxic positivity, forced gratitude, comparative framing

### 5. Notification Tiers
- Days 1-7: Daily (habit anchoring window)
- Days 8-30: Every 2 days
- 30+ days: User-set cadence
- Lapsed: Day 3, 7, 14 then stop

## QuitSure Patterns to Adopt
- Structured time-boxed programs (7/14/21 days) > open-ended tools
- Belief restructuring before behavior change
- "Keep doing what you're doing" observation period
- Daily drip content creates ritual
- SOS button for when old patterns hit
- Reframe growth as liberation not discipline
