# QuitSure Research — What Mirror Can Learn

## QuitSure's Method
- **Cognitive restructuring** (inspired by Allen Carr's "Easy Way")
- 6-day structured program: Days 1-5 keep smoking + watch belief-dismantling videos, Day 6 quit
- User continues the unwanted behavior during the program — removes resistance
- 95% of completers attempt to quit, 50-70% stay smoke-free

## Key Principles for Mirror

### 1. Belief restructuring BEFORE behavior change
Don't ask "go to the gym." First dismantle "I'm lazy" / "I have no discipline" / "I'm not a gym person." When the belief shifts, action follows naturally.

### 2. "Keep doing what you're doing" onboarding
Mirror should have an observation period: "For the first week, don't try to change anything. Just talk to me and let me understand you." Removes pressure, builds trust, creates engagement.

### 3. Time-boxed structured programs > open-ended tools
Offer 7-day / 14-day / 21-day programs:
- "7 Days to Understanding Your Energy Patterns"
- "14 Days to Dismantling Self-Criticism"
- "21 Days to Building Your First Tiny Habit"

### 4. Daily drip content creates ritual
One insight per day, unlocked sequentially. Creates anticipation and investment.

### 5. Real-time intervention tool (SOS button)
When old patterns hit (procrastination, negative self-talk), tap a button → get a short targeted reframe specific to beliefs they've already worked through.

### 6. Reframe from deprivation to liberation
Growth is "becoming who you already are", not "grinding through discipline." Frame change as gaining freedom, not losing comfort.

### 7. Progressive revelation builds investment
Each day reveals a new insight → "sunk cost of understanding" → users don't want to stop partway.

## Implementation Ideas for Mirror
- [ ] Structured onboarding program (7 days, belief-focused)
- [ ] SOS/craving button for negative self-talk patterns
- [ ] Daily insight system (one per day, progressive)
- [ ] Observation period before action suggestions
- [ ] Identity-level reframing ("you're someone who..." not "you should...")
